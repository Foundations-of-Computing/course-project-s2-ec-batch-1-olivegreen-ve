  • Towed Howitzers: Bofors FH77, Dhanush Howitzer

  • Main Battle Tanks (MBTs): Arjun Mk-1A, T-90 Bhishma, T-72 Ajeya

    • Weapons and Aircraft Used by The Indian Air Force (IAF)

  • 1.Fighter Aircraft
  • Multirole Fighters: Dassault Rafale, Sukhoi Su-30MKI, HAL Tejas Mk-1, Mirage 2000
  • Air Superiority Fighters: MiG-29UPG
  • Ground Attack Aircraft: SEPECAT Jaguar
  • Interceptor Fighters: MiG-21 Bison (being phased out)
    • 2.Transport Aircraft
  • Strategic Transport: Boeing C-17 Globemaster III, Ilyushin Il-76
  • Tactical Transport: C-130J Super Hercules, Antonov An-32
  • Utility Aircraft: Dornier Do-228, Boeing 737 (for VIP transport)
    • 3.Aerial Refueling & AWACS
  • Airborne Early Warning and Control (AEW&C): DRDO Netra, Beriev A-50EI
  • Aerial Refueling Tankers: Ilyushin Il-78MKI
    • 4.Helicopters
  • Attack Helicopters: Boeing AH-64E Apache, HAL Rudra
  • Transport & Utility Helicopters: HAL Dhruv, Mi-17V-5, Mi-26, Chetak, Cheetah
  • Heavy Lift Helicopters: CH-47F Chinook
    • 5.Drones & UAVs
  • Surveillance UAVs: Heron, Searcher Mk-II
  • Combat UAVs (Upcoming): Predator MQ-9B Reaper (to be acquired), Archer-NG
    • 6.Missile Systems
  • Air-to-Air Missiles (AAMs): Short-Range: R-73, Python-5, Astra Mk-1 Medium/Long-Range: Meteor, MICA, R-77, Astra Mk-2 (under development)
  • Air-to-Surface Missiles (ASMs): BrahMos-A (air-launched version) Spice-2000 (used in Balakot airstrike) DRDO Smart Anti-Airfield Weapon (SAAW)
  • Anti-Radiation Missiles (ARMs): Rudram-1 (under testing)
    • Air Defence & Radar Systems
  • Surface-to-Air Missiles (SAMs): Akash Missile System, SPYDER, Barak-8
  • Ballistic Missile Defence (BMD): Prithvi Air Defence (PAD), Advanced Air Defence (AAD)
  • Radar Systems: Rohini Radar, Indra Radar, EL/M-2083 AEW radar

    • Indian Navy Fleets & Warships

    1.Fleets and Vessels:
    Aircraft Carriers
  • INS Vikrant: India's first domestically-built aircraft carrier, commissioned in 2022
  • INS Vikramaditya: A modified Kiev-class aircraft carrier serving as the flagship of the Indian Navy.
    • Destroyers:
  • Visakhapatnam-class (Project 15B): INS Visakhapatnam: Commissioned in 2021. INS Mormugao: Commissioned in 2022. INS Imphal: Commissioned in 2023. INS Surat: Commissioned on January 15, 2025
  • Frigates
  • Nilgiri-class (Project 17A): INS Nilgiri: Commissioned on January 15, 2025. INS Himgiri: Expected delivery in Q2 of FY2025-26. INS Udaygiri: Potential delivery by end of FY2024-25. INS Dunagiri: Under construction.
  • Talwar-class (Follow-on): INS Tushil: Commissioned on December 9, 2024. Additional units: Two under construction at Goa Shipyard Limited (GSL) with expected deliveries in 2026 and 2027.
    • Submarines:
  • Kalvari-class (Scorpene): INS Kalvari: Commissioned in 2017. INS Khanderi: Commissioned in 2019. INS Karanj: Commissioned in 2021. INS Vela: Commissioned in 2021. INS Vagir: Commissioned in 2022. INS Vagsheer: Delivered on January 9, 2025, and commissioned on January 15, 2025
    • Corvettes:
  • Kamorta-class (Project 28): Four units commissioned between 2014 and 2020.
    • Survey Vessels:
  • INS Sandhayak: Delivered in December 2023. INS Nirdeshak: Expected delivery in late August or early September 2025. INS Ikshak and INS Sanshodhak: Scheduled for delivery in March and June 2025, respectively.
    • 2.Weapon Systems
    Missiles:
  • BrahMos Supersonic Cruise Missiles: Integrated into various platforms, including destroyers and frigates, enhancing strike capabilities.
  • Barak-8 Surface-to-Air Missiles: Deployed on multiple vessels for robust air defense.
  • Hypersonic Missiles: Successfully tested, capable of carrying payloads over 1,500 km at speeds exceeding Mach 5.
    • Naval guns and close in weapos systems:
  • 76mm Super Rapid Gun Mount (SRGM)
  • AK-630M CIWS: Installed on various vessels for point defense against aerial threats.
    • Torpedoes and Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW) Systems:
  • L&T Indigenous Rocket Launchers: Deployed for ASW operations
  • Wire-guided Torpedoes and Exocet Anti-ship Missiles: Arming submarines like the INS Vagsheer.

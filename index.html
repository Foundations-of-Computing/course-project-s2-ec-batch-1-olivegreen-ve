TRI-SERVICE

  • The Indian Armed Forces consist of three main branches: the Indian Army, Indian Navy, and Indian Air Force, collectively known as the Tri-Services. The Indian Army is responsible for land-based operations, protecting the country’s borders and maintaining internal security. The Indian Navy safeguards India's maritime interests, ensuring security in the Indian Ocean region and conducting naval operations. The Indian Air Force provides aerial defense, strategic bombing, and air support to ground and naval forces. These three services operate under the Ministry of Defence and are led by their respective chiefs, with overall coordination handled by the Chief of Defence Staff (CDS). The Integrated Defence Staff (IDS) ensures joint operations and synergy among the forces, enhancing India's military preparedness and response capabilities.
